THE MIDNIGHT LINE
Surreal urban fantasy on Aussie trains after dark
Core Pitch
After the last normal train stops, your line keeps going.
From 12:01am to first light, the train carries:
* souls and dreams,
* half-finished prayers,
* regrets that got left on voicemails,
* and people who feel “stuck” without knowing why.
You’re the crew of this midnight service.
Your job isn’t to fight monsters.
Your job is to quietly heal one person’s story… and they’ll never know you did.
________________


Who the Game Is “For”
At the start of each run, the table chooses one person:
* a burnt-out nurse on day shift,
* a grieving dad,
* a teenager who feels invisible,
* a mum scrolling her phone in the dark,
* a man on the verge of walking out.
You write 3 prompts about them:
* What they’re scared of.
* What they deeply want.
* What they’d never say out loud.
You never meet them directly.
The whole session is heaven’s “night-shift crew” working on their heart.
________________


________________
Player Roles
You’re not ordinary humans. You might be:
* Conductors – keep the timetable of fate, punch metaphysical tickets.
* Gatherers – collect unsent messages and lost thoughts.
* Signalers – flip spiritual “points” to change where someone’s life tracks go.
* Wipers – clean windows so people can see clearly for just a moment.
* Buskers – songs, stories, or jokes that shift the emotional weather.
Give each PC:
* A Role (above).
* A Uniform Quirk (e.g. hi-vis that glows with people’s worries; lanyard that lists their past lives; ticket punch that cuts out words).
* A Private Wound that secretly mirrors the person you’re helping.

Stations as States of Heart
Each station is an emotional landscape, not just a place.
Some examples:
* REGRET

   * Platforms cracked like old photo frames.
   * Announcements glitch: “Next stop: If Only… If Only…”
   * Encounters: ghosts of choices-not-taken, NPCs who want to rerun the same moment forever.
   * BEGINNING AGAIN

      * Graffiti slowly repainting itself into fresh colours.
      * New saplings growing through the old asphalt.
      * Encounters: people stepping off the wrong train trying to get back on track.
      * UNSENT MESSAGES

         * Air full of text bubbles and unsent emails floating like lanterns.
         * Encounters: letters that chase you, voicemails that beg to be heard.
         * THINGS I NEVER SAID TO MY DAD

            * Platform benches like old loungerooms.
            * TV screens showing footy games with the sound turned down.
            * Encounters: “Dads” you can speak through, or confront, or forgive.
You can add more:
            * NUMB SCROLLING, SHAME, FIRST LOVE, HOSPITAL NIGHT SHIFT, CHURCH CAR PARK, etc.

Core Mechanics (System-Agnostic)
You can bolt this onto D&D, PBTA, or a custom system. Core loop:
            1. Choose the Person – write their 3 prompts.
            2. Choose 3–4 Stations – emotional beats that feel like their journey.
            3. At each station, present:
            * 1 Scene that expresses their inner world.
            * 1–2 Choices for how the crew intervenes (talk, re-route, reveal, comfort, confront).
            4. Impact Track:
            * Have a simple track from Stuck → Wavering → Softened → Open → Turning Point.
            * Good choices nudge it right, unhelpful ones nudge it left.
            5. At the end of the run, you briefly narrate:
            * A small, grounded change in their daytime life.
            * e.g. they text someone, they book counselling, they turn back from the door, they finally sleep.
Combat:
            * Rare, metaphorical.
            * “Monsters” are things like:
            * The Algorithm (keeps people numb).
            * The Old Script (“you’re useless”, “you’re too much”).
            * The Fog (dissociation).
Winning isn’t killing them; it’s breaking their grip on the passenger’s story.

1. High-Level Concept
Title: The Midnight Line
Genre: Narrative adventure / emotional roguelite / light RPG
Perspective: 2.5D side-on exploration on the train and platforms (like Spiritfarer/Oxenfree vibes).
Tone: Surreal urban fantasy, gentle horror of the heart, hopeful ending. Aussie specificity.
Core Fantasy:
 You’re the night-shift crew of a secret after-hours train line. Every night, from 12:01am to first light, you quietly intervene in one stranger’s life by travelling through stations that are their inner world. You never meet them directly. You just nudge their heart.
Winning isn’t “beating” anything. It’s that the next morning, in the real world, they take one small, holy step: sending a text, calling someone back, walking back through the door.

2. Pillars (What Makes It AAA-Indie-Worthy)
               1. Deep, Replayable Emotional Stories

                  * Each run = a different person (burnt-out nurse, grieving dad, teen, mum in the dark, man about to leave).

                  * Their Fear / Deep Desire / Unspoken Secret drive the whole night’s stations and events.

                  * Combo of authored story arcs and procedural remixing for longevity.

                     2. Crew-Based Play

                        * You control a small crew (e.g. 2–3 active characters on a run):

                           * Conductor – fate/timetable manipulation.

                           * Gatherer – collects unsent messages, memory fragments.

                           * Signaler – switches “life tracks” at key moments.

                           * Wiper – clears perception (windows, mirrors, screens).

                           * Busker – shifts emotional climate with stories and songs.

                              * Each has abilities, skill trees, and a Private Wound that thematically mirrors the night’s passenger.

                                 3. Stations as Emotional Biomes

                                    * Fully-realised, animated stations like:

                                       * REGRET, UNSENT MESSAGES, HOSPITAL NIGHT SHIFT, CHURCH CAR PARK, NUMB SCROLLING, THINGS I NEVER SAID TO MY DAD, etc.

                                          * Each biome has:

                                             * A distinct visual grammar.

                                             * Its own “monsters” (metaphors like The Algorithm, The Old Script, The Fog).

                                             * Environmental puzzles and dialog scenes that embody one emotional beat.

                                                4. The Impact Track (Heart-State System)

                                                   * A visible but diegetic gauge (e.g. a driver’s console with an old analog dial) from:

                                                      * Stuck → Wavering → Softened → Open → Turning Point

                                                         * Every intervention nudges it.

                                                         * Push too hard? It can snap back or shut down.

                                                            5. Non-Violent “Combat”

                                                               * Encounters with The Algorithm, Old Script, Fog etc.

                                                               * Resolved with:

                                                                  * Choices of words.

                                                                  * Timing.

                                                                  * Use of crew abilities.

                                                                  * Sacrificing comfort/routine to offer truth or rest.

                                                                     * You “win” by loosening their grip, not killing them.

                                                                           * Conductor adjusts the timetable: slowing/speeding the night, reordering stations.

                                                                           * Gatherer pulls unsent messages out of phones and air.

                                                                              * Choose next station from a branching rail map—each choice implies a different emotional beat.

                                                                                             3. Key Encounter

                                                                                                * An echo of the Person (or someone representing them: a Dad, a ward nurse, a church elder, a schoolyard).

                                                                                                            * Visuals subtly change (lights brighten, graffiti repainting, fog thinning).

5. Player Roles as Fully-Fledged Playstyles
Each role isn’t just flavour—they change how you solve problems.
                                                                                                                                                            * Conductor (Control / Planning)

                                                                                                                                                               * Abilities that reorder stations, delay/advance certain events, and “hold” the night a bit longer.
                                                                                                                                                               * Skill tree: Timetable (macro route control), Tickets (1:1 interventions), Delays (slowing down harmful scripts).

                                                                                                                                                                  * Gatherer (Memory / Info)

                                                                                                                                                                     * Sees floating message fragments others can’t.
                                                                                                                                                                     * Can reconstruct key scenes from scattered clues.
                                                                                                                                                                     * Skill tree: Inbox (texts & emails), Archive (old memories), Overflow (church prayers, journal entries).

                                                                                                                                                                        * Signaler (Branching Paths)

                                                                                                                                                                           * Interacts with “junctions” in the Person’s life.

                                                                                                                                                                           * Can temporarily open alternate timelines/what-ifs.

                                                                                                                                                                           * Skill tree: Points (binary choices), Divergence (hidden third paths), Reconnect (relink broken relationships).

                                                                                                                                                                              * Wiper (Clarity / Perception)

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Wipes mist from windows, grime from mirrors, static from screens.

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Reveals hidden NPC motives or lies.

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Skill tree: Glass (external clarity), Mirror (self-clarity), Screen (media/algorithm clarity).

                                                                                                                                                                                    * Busker (Emotion / Atmosphere)

                                                                                                                                                                                       * Live “AOE” emotional shifts: calming anxious mobs, emboldening a scared echo, softening a hard crowd.

                                                                                                                                                                                       * Skill tree: Songs (mood), Stories (perspective), Banter (disarming defences).

You choose a party each run; synergy between your crew shapes how you approach healing.

                                                                                                                                                                                          * Unique music themes & sound design.

                                                                                                                                                                                          * Local NPC archetypes.

Example:
HOSPITAL NIGHT SHIFT
                                                                                                                                                                                             * White noise, buzzing fluorescents, beeping monitors.

                                                                                                                                                                                             * NPCs: numb nurses, parent-shadows, exhausted doctors.

                                                                                                                                                                                             * Monsters: The Clipboard (endless tasks), The Code Blue (flashbacks), The Never-Empty Waiting Room.

                                                                                                                                                                                             * Puzzles: Do you give a nurse rest, or help a parent stay present? THINGS I NEVER SAID TO MY DAD

All of this is designed to be shippable at indie AAA quality—so fewer, richer biomes rather than endless shallow ones.
